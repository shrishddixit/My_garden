# PARIJATA CARE GUIDE 🌼🤍  
(Nyctanthes arbor-tristis — Night Flowering Jasmine / Coral Jasmine)

---

## 🌳 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Parijata is a **small tree / large shrub with seasonal fragrant blooms**.

Core traits:

✔ Tree physiology (NOT shrub like hibiscus)  
✔ Flowers seasonally (often heavy flushes)  
✔ Extremely sunlight responsive ☀️  
✔ Moderate feeder  
✔ Sensitive to overwatering ❌  
✔ Hardy once established  
✔ Minimal pruning required  
✔ Flowers on NEW GROWTH  

Critical mental model:

Parijata = Stability + Sunlight + Moderate nutrition.

Too much pampering → Leaf-heavy / Flower-poor  
Too little care → Slow growth / Weak flowering

---

## ☀️ LIGHT REQUIREMENT (MOST IMPORTANT)

Parijata LOVES sun.

✔ Minimum 6–8 hrs direct sunlight  
✔ Full sun ideal

Low light symptoms:

✔ Healthy leaves ✅  
✔ Very few/no flowers ❌  
✔ Thin weak stems  
✔ Poor fragrance

Sunlight = Primary flowering trigger.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT

Ideal soil behaviour:

✔ Well-draining (CRITICAL)  
✔ Loose & aerated  
✔ Moderately rich  
✔ Never compacted

BIGGEST PARIJATA KILLER:

❌ Wet soil → Root stress → Yellow leaves → Leaf drop → Flower failure

---

### ✅ ORGANIC FOUNDATION

Every 45–60 days:

✔ Vermicompost → 300–500 g  
✔ Cow Manure → Light handful

Why:

✔ Sustained nutrition  
✔ Supports flowering cycles  
✔ Improves soil vitality

Optional booster:

✔ Humic Acid Drench

Dose:
2–3 g/L

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days

Why:

✔ Root stimulation  
✔ Better nutrient uptake

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY (VERY IMPORTANT)

Young plant:

✔ 2–3 times per week

Established tree:

✔ Deep watering every 5–7 days

Golden rule:

✔ Allow partial drying  
✔ NEVER constant wet soil  
✔ Deep watering preferred

Classic failure:

✘ Daily watering ❌

Symptoms of overwatering:

✔ Yellow leaves  
✔ Leaf drop  
✔ Weak growth  
✔ No flowers ❌

Underwatering symptoms:

✔ Leaf droop (usually recovers fast)

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (TREE PHYSIOLOGY)

Parijata = Moderate feeder.

Overfeeding is VERY common mistake.

---

### 🌿 GENERAL GROWTH PHASE

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 20–30 days

Why:

✔ Balanced canopy growth  
✔ Leaf & root support

Excess nitrogen → Leaves ↑ Flowers ↓

---

### 🌼 PRE-FLOWERING / FLOWERING PHASE

✔ SOP 00:00:50 (Potassium Support)

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 20–30 days

Why:

✔ Flower initiation  
✔ Bud formation  
✔ Fragrance intensity  
✔ Flower longevity

Potassium = Flower stabilizer.

---

### 🦴 CALCIUM SUPPORT (OPTIONAL)

✔ Calcium Nitrate

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Stem strength  
✔ Bud durability

Do NOT mix with SOP.

---

### 🌿 MAGNESIUM BOOST (IMPORTANT)

✔ Epsom Salt

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days

Why:

✔ Leaf colour  
✔ Photosynthesis efficiency

Magnesium deficiency common in container plants.

---

### 🔬 MICRONUTRIENTS (IMPORTANT)

✔ Micronutrient Mix

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days

Why:

✔ Prevents hidden deficiencies  
✔ Supports flowering

Deficiency symptoms:

✔ Pale leaves  
✔ Weak flowering  
✔ Small flowers

---

### 🌊 SEAWEED EXTRACT (EXCELLENT)

Dose:
2–3 ml/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Stress buffering  
✔ Flower stimulation  
✔ Root vigor

Parijata responds well to seaweed.

---

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT

Generally hardy but can attract:

✔ Aphids  
✔ Mealybugs  
✔ Caterpillars  
✔ Scale insects

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE BASELINE

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3–5 ml/L + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 15–20 days

Increase if pests visible.

---

### ✅ SAP SUCKING PESTS

✔ Thiamethoxam (if severe)

---

### ✅ CHEWING / CATERPILLARS

✔ Indoxacarb / Chlorantraniliprole (rotate)

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Common issues:

✔ Leaf spot (rare)  
✔ Root rot (watering issue)

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE

✔ Hexaconazole

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days (humid/rainy season)

---

### ✅ CURATIVE

✔ Azoxystrobin + Mancozeb

Use when spotting visible.

---

## ✂️ PRUNING STRATEGY (MINIMALIST)

Parijata ≠ Aggressive pruning plant.

Over-pruning reduces flowering.

---

### ✅ WHEN TO PRUNE

✔ Only when needed  
✔ After flowering season  
✔ Structural corrections

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Dead branches  
✔ Diseased wood  
✔ Crossing branches  
✔ Weak growth

Goal:

✔ Natural canopy  
✔ Good airflow  
✔ Light penetration

---

### ✅ CUTTING TECHNIQUE

✔ Cut outside branch collar  
✔ Slight angled cut  
✔ Clean sharp tool  
✔ No stubs

---

## 🌼 FLOWERING INSIGHTS (IMPORTANT REALITY)

Parijata flowering is:

✔ Seasonal  
✔ Flush-based  
✔ Influenced by climate & maturity

No flowers ≠ Problem.

Often normal.

MOST COMMON REASONS FOR POOR FLOWERING:

✔ Low sunlight ☀️  
✔ Excess nitrogen 🌿  
✔ Overwatering 💧  
✔ Plant immaturity 🌱  
✔ Potassium deficiency 🧪  
✔ Micronutrient deficiency 🔬  

---

## 🌼 LEAF DROP (COMMON PARIJATA BEHAVIOUR)

Some leaf shedding is NORMAL.

Heavy leaf drop usually indicates:

✔ Overwatering  
✔ Root stress  
✔ Sudden environmental change

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Parijata struggles mostly from:

1) Insufficient sunlight (MOST COMMON)  
2) Overwatering  
3) Excess nitrogen  
4) Poor drainage  
5) Impatience (seasonal bloomer)  
6) Heavy pruning  
7) Nutrient imbalance

---

## 🎯 OPTIMAL CARE PHILOSOPHY

Parijata thrives on:

✔ Full sunlight dominance ☀️  
✔ Excellent drainage 🌱  
✔ Deep spaced watering 💧  
✔ Moderate balanced feeding 🌿  
✔ Potassium support during flowering  
✔ Periodic micronutrients 🔬  
✔ Minimal intelligent pruning ✂️  
✔ Stable environment  
✔ Good airflow  
✔ Patience

Parijata is a rhythm plant.

Stable cycles → Heavy bloom flushes.

Over-management → Growth confusion.
