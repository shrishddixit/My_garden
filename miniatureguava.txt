# MINIATURE GUAVA CARE GUIDE
(Container / dwarf-type guava management strategy

---

## 🌳 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Miniature guava ≠ Normal guava scaled down.

It behaves like a **high-density fruiting shrub in restricted root volume**.

Critical biological differences:

✔ Root space limited → Nutrition precision required  
✔ Faster nutrient depletion  
✔ Faster stress response  
✔ Higher pruning dependency  
✔ Watering mistakes amplified  
✔ Fruits on NEW GROWTH (same as regular guava)

Mental model:

Container plants live in an artificial ecosystem you fully control.

No soil buffer. No forgiveness.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT (EXTREMELY IMPORTANT)

Container soil degrades over time.

Ideal mix characteristics:

✔ Fast draining  
✔ Highly aerated  
✔ Nutrient rich  
✔ Never compacted

---

### ✅ ORGANIC FOUNDATION

Every 30–45 days (more frequent than trees):

✔ Cow Manure → Small handful (VERY LIGHT)  
✔ Vermicompost → 200–300 g

Why reduced quantity:

✔ Excess organics → Poor drainage → Root suffocation  
✔ Container soil clogs easily

Critical rule:

✔ Always loosen topsoil gently before adding compost

---

### ✅ HUMIC ACID (HIGHLY BENEFICIAL IN POTS)

Dose:
2 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30 days

Why:

✔ Prevents salt buildup  
✔ Enhances nutrient absorption  
✔ Reduces root stress

Container plants LOVE humic acid.

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY (BIGGEST FAILURE POINT)

Golden rule:

✔ Moist — NOT wet  
✔ Dry slightly — NOT bone dry

Typical rhythm:

• Every 2–4 days depending on heat

Test method (best practice):

✔ Insert finger 2 inches deep  
✔ Water only if dry

Classic container killers:

✘ Daily watering → Root rot  
✘ Full drying → Flower drop / fruit drop

Correct approach:

Hydration rhythm, not habit.

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (PRECISION FEEDING)

Miniature guava consumes nutrients faster.

---

### 🌿 GENERAL GROWTH PHASE

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
0.5 – 1 g/L (slightly lighter)

Frequency:
Every 10–15 days

Why:

✔ Maintains steady growth  
✔ Prevents depletion shock

---

### 🍈 FLOWERING & FRUITING PHASE (MOST IMPORTANT)

✔ SOP 00:00:50

Dose:
0.5 – 1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Why:

✔ Fruit size  
✔ Sweetness  
✔ Prevents flower drop

Optimal pattern:

Alternate:

NPK → SOP → NPK → SOP

Container plants benefit from tighter cycles.

---

### 🦴 CALCIUM SUPPORT (CRITICAL IN CONTAINERS)

✔ Calcium Nitrate

Dose:
0.5 – 1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 20–25 days

Why:

✔ Prevents fruit cracking  
✔ Improves fruit firmness  
✔ Prevents stress-related drop

Do NOT mix with SOP.

---

### 🌿 MAGNESIUM BOOST

✔ Epsom Salt

Dose:
0.5 – 1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30 days

Why:

✔ Prevents leaf yellowing  
✔ Enhances chlorophyll

Containers often develop magnesium deficiency.

---

### 🔬 MICRONUTRIENTS (VERY IMPORTANT)

✔ Micronutrient Mix

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Prevents chronic deficiency  
✔ Ensures proper fruit formation

Deficiency appears faster in pots.

---

### 🌊 SEAWEED EXTRACT (EXCELLENT FOR DWARF FRUITERS)

Dose:
2 ml/L

Frequency:
Every 20–30 days

Why:

✔ Stress buffering  
✔ Flower stimulation  
✔ Root vigor

---

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT (MORE VULNERABLE IN POTS)

Common pests identical to guava but impact is faster.

• Aphids  
• Mealybugs  
• Whiteflies  
• Fruit flies  
• Mites (sometimes)

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE BASELINE (ESSENTIAL)

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3 ml/L + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Container plants need preventive stability.

---

### ✅ SAP SUCKING PESTS

✔ Thiamethoxam

Use only when visible infestation.

Containers magnify chemical stress.

Avoid overuse.

---

### ✅ CATERPILLARS / CHEWERS

✔ Chlorantraniliprole  
✔ Indoxacarb

Rotation principle remains critical.

---

## 🚨 FRUIT FLY (IMPORTANT EVEN FOR MINIATURE)

✔ Remove fallen fruits immediately  
✔ Neem oil preventive sprays help

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Containers reduce fungal risk slightly but not immunity.

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE

✔ Hexaconazole

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days (humid season)

---

### ✅ CURATIVE / STRONG PROTECTION

✔ Azoxystrobin + Mancozeb

Use when spotting appears.

---

## ✂️ PRUNING STRATEGY (EXTREMELY IMPORTANT)

Miniature guava MUST be shaped actively.

Goal:

✔ Compact productive shrub  
✔ New fruiting wood stimulation  
✔ Height & density control

---

### ✅ WHEN TO PRUNE

✔ After harvest  
✔ Whenever structure becomes dense  
✔ Light shaping anytime

---

### ✅ PRUNING OBJECTIVES

✔ Maintain size  
✔ Stimulate branching  
✔ Encourage new shoots  
✔ Prevent canopy congestion

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Weak thin shoots  
✔ Inward growth  
✔ Crossing branches  
✔ Excess height leaders  
✔ Old unproductive wood

Miniature guava benefits from **frequent light pruning**.

---

### ✅ IDEAL STRUCTURE

✔ Dense but airy bush  
✔ Sunlight penetration  
✔ Multiple fruiting points

---

### ✅ CUTTING TECHNIQUE

✔ Cut above outward-facing node  
✔ Slight angled cut  
✔ Clean pruner  
✔ No stubs

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS (Container Specific)

Miniature guava struggles mostly from:

1) Overwatering (MOST COMMON)  
2) Soil compaction  
3) Excess organics → Poor drainage  
4) Irregular fertilization  
5) No pruning → Crowded canopy  
6) Salt buildup from fertilizers

Humic acid helps prevent #6.

---

## 🎯 OPTIMAL CARE PHILOSOPHY

Miniature guava thrives on:

✔ Precise light feeding cycles  
✔ Controlled watering rhythm  
✔ Aggressive structural shaping  
✔ Excellent drainage  
✔ Micronutrient vigilance  
✔ Preventive neem protection

Container plants reward consistency more than intensity.

Small root zone → Small margin of error.
