# LEMON CARE GUIDE 🍋  
Complete nutrition + protection + pruning strategy

---

## 🌳 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Lemon is a high-energy, high-demand citrus plant.

Core traits:

✔ Heavy feeder (nutrient hungry)  
✔ Extremely sensitive to deficiencies  
✔ Fruits multiple times per year  
✔ Sensitive to overwatering  
✔ Loves sunlight  
✔ Requires structured pruning  
✔ Shallow but aggressive roots

Critical mental model:

Citrus plants are metabolic athletes.

Small imbalance → Visible drama.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT (CRITICAL FOR CITRUS)

Ideal soil:

✔ Fast draining (NON-NEGOTIABLE)  
✔ Slightly acidic preferred (pH ~5.5–6.5)  
✔ Rich organic matter  
✔ Highly aerated

Citrus hates:

✘ Waterlogged soil  
✘ Compacted soil  
✘ Heavy clay without aeration

---

### ✅ ORGANIC FOUNDATION

Every 45–60 days:

✔ Cow Manure → 1–2 kg (tree) / light handful (pot)  
✔ Vermicompost → 500 g – 1 kg

Why:

✔ Sustained nutrient base  
✔ Improves soil biology  
✔ Prevents micronutrient lockout

Optional booster:

✔ Humic Acid Drench

Dose:
2–3 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Root stimulation  
✔ Nutrient absorption boost  
✔ Reduces salt buildup (important in pots)

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY (BIGGEST FAILURE POINT)

Golden rule:

✔ Moist — NOT wet  
✔ Allow partial drying between watering

Young plant:

• 2–3 times per week

Established tree:

• Deep watering every 4–6 days

Classic citrus killer:

✘ Daily watering → Root rot → Leaf yellowing

Symptoms of overwatering:

✔ Yellow leaves  
✔ Leaf drop  
✔ Weak growth  
✔ No flowering

Correct strategy:

Deep, spaced watering.

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (HIGH PRECISION REQUIRED)

Lemon = Heavy feeder + Constant producer.

Nutrition errors show quickly.

---

### 🌿 GENERAL GROWTH PHASE

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 15–20 days

Why:

✔ Balanced canopy development  
✔ Leaf + shoot growth  
✔ Root support

---

### 🍋 FLOWERING & FRUITING PHASE (MOST IMPORTANT)

✔ SOP 00:00:50 (Potassium Dominant)

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 10–15 days

Why:

✔ Flower induction  
✔ Fruit size  
✔ Juice quality  
✔ Stress resistance  
✔ Prevents flower drop

Optimal rhythm:

Alternate:

NPK → SOP → NPK → SOP

---

### 🦴 CALCIUM SUPPORT (VERY IMPORTANT)

✔ Calcium Nitrate

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 25–30 days

Why:

✔ Prevents fruit cracking  
✔ Improves fruit firmness  
✔ Reduces fruit drop

Do NOT mix with SOP.

---

### 🌿 MAGNESIUM BOOST (CITRUS LOVES THIS)

✔ Epsom Salt

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30 days

Why:

✔ Prevents leaf yellowing  
✔ Deep green foliage  
✔ Better photosynthesis

Magnesium deficiency is EXTREMELY common in citrus.

---

### 🔬 MICRONUTRIENTS (CRITICAL FOR LEMON)

Citrus is micronutrient obsessed.

✔ Micronutrient Mix

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Prevents chlorosis  
✔ Ensures flowering  
✔ Supports fruit formation

Deficiency symptoms:

✔ Yellow leaves with green veins  
✔ Small leaves  
✔ Poor flowering  
✔ Weak fruits

Ignoring micronutrients = Chronic lemon misery.

---

### 🌊 SEAWEED EXTRACT (Highly Beneficial)

Dose:
2–3 ml/L

Frequency:
Every 20–30 days

Why:

✔ Flower stimulation  
✔ Stress tolerance  
✔ Root vigor  
✔ Fruit retention

---

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT (VERY IMPORTANT FOR CITRUS)

Common pests:

• Aphids  
• Leaf miners (VERY COMMON)  
• Mealybugs  
• Scale insects  
• Whiteflies

---

## 🚨 LEAF MINER (CITRUS CLASSIC ENEMY)

Symptoms:

✔ Curled distorted leaves  
✔ Silvery trails  
✔ Ugly new growth

Damage:

✔ Reduces photosynthesis  
✔ Weakens plant  
✔ Slows growth

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE BASELINE (ESSENTIAL)

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3–5 ml/L + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Especially important during new leaf flush.

Neem is VERY effective against leaf miners.

---

### ✅ SAP SUCKING PESTS

✔ Thiamethoxam

Targets:

✔ Aphids  
✔ Mealybugs  
✔ Whiteflies

Use only when infestation visible.

---

### ✅ CHEWING / LARVAL PESTS

✔ Chlorantraniliprole  
✔ Indoxacarb

Rotate chemistry.

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Common citrus diseases:

• Leaf spot  
• Sooty mold  
• Powdery mildew (occasionally)

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE

✔ Hexaconazole

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days (humid weather)

---

### ✅ STRONG PROTECTION

✔ Azoxystrobin + Mancozeb

Use when spotting visible.

---

## ✂️ PRUNING STRATEGY (CRITICAL FOR YIELD)

Lemon pruning = Structured management, not aggressive cutting.

Goal:

✔ Balanced canopy  
✔ Light penetration  
✔ Airflow  
✔ Fruit-bearing wood stimulation

---

### ✅ WHEN TO PRUNE

✔ After harvest  
✔ Before major growth flush  
✔ Avoid heavy pruning during fruiting

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Dead branches  
✔ Diseased wood  
✔ Crossing branches  
✔ Inward-growing shoots  
✔ Rootstock suckers (VERY IMPORTANT)

---

## 🚨 ROOTSTOCK SUCKERS (CRITICAL CITRUS RULE)

Symptoms:

✔ Fast vigorous shoots from base  
✔ Different leaf shape  
✔ Thorny aggressive growth

Problem:

✔ Steals nutrients  
✔ Suppresses fruiting

Action:

✔ REMOVE immediately

---

### ✅ STRUCTURAL OBJECTIVE

✔ Open airy canopy  
✔ Sunlight penetration  
✔ Manageable height

Dense citrus canopy = Pest + disease paradise.

---

### ✅ CUTTING TECHNIQUE

✔ Cut above outward-facing node  
✔ Slight angled cut  
✔ Clean sharp pruner  
✔ No stubs

---

## 🍋 FLOWERING & FRUITING INSIGHTS

Common problems & causes:

No flowers → Excess nitrogen / Low potassium  
Flowers drop → Water stress / Potassium deficiency  
Small fruits → Potassium deficiency  
Yellow leaves → Magnesium / Micronutrient deficiency  
Thick peel / low juice → Potassium imbalance  
Leaf curl → Leaf miners / Water stress

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Lemon struggles mostly from:

1) Overwatering (MOST COMMON)  
2) Magnesium deficiency (EXTREMELY COMMON)  
3) Micronutrient neglect  
4) Potassium deficiency  
5) Ignored leaf miners  
6) Rootstock suckers stealing energy

Lemon thrives on:

✔ Excellent drainage  
✔ Balanced NPK rhythm  
✔ Potassium dominance during flowering  
✔ Aggressive micronutrient support  
✔ Regular magnesium supplementation  
✔ Preventive neem protection

✔ Strong sunlight exposure

Citrus rewards precision & consistency.

Small errors → Loud symptoms.
