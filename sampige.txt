# SAMPIGE CARE GUIDE 🌼  
(Magnolia champaca / Champak — Fragrant Flowering Tree)

---

## 🌳 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Sampige (champaca) is a **slow-growing evergreen fragrant tree**, not a shrub.

Core traits:

✔ Tree physiology (different from jasmine/rose)  
✔ Slow but steady growth  
✔ Deep-rooted once established  
✔ Flowers seasonally (not continuous bloomer)  
✔ Sensitive to root disturbance  
✔ Moderate feeder  
✔ Minimal pruning requirement  
✔ Strongly sunlight responsive

Critical mental model:

Champaca is a patience plant.

It prioritizes structure & root establishment before heavy flowering.

---

## ☀️ LIGHT REQUIREMENT (CRITICAL)

Sampige LOVES sunlight.

✔ Minimum 6–8 hrs direct sunlight  
✔ Full sun ideal

Low light symptoms:

✔ Healthy leaves  
✔ Very few/no flowers ❌  
✔ Slow growth  
✔ Weak fragrance

Sunlight = Primary flowering trigger.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT

Ideal soil behaviour:

✔ Well-draining (NON-NEGOTIABLE)  
✔ Deep & aerated  
✔ Rich organic matter  
✔ Slightly acidic preferred

Champaca roots HATE:

✘ Waterlogging  
✘ Heavy compacted soil  
✘ Frequent root disturbance

---

### ✅ ORGANIC FOUNDATION

Every 60 days:

✔ Cow Manure → 1–2 kg (tree)  
✔ Vermicompost → 500 g – 1 kg

Why:

✔ Sustained nutrition  
✔ Soil vitality  
✔ Supports slow metabolism

Optional booster:

✔ Humic Acid Drench

Dose:
2–3 g/L

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days

Why:

✔ Root system stimulation  
✔ Improves nutrient uptake

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY

Young plant:

✔ 2–3 times per week

Established tree:

✔ Deep watering every 5–7 days

Golden rule:

✔ Moist — NOT soggy  
✔ Avoid frequent shallow watering

Classic champaca killer:

✘ Constant wet soil → Root stress → Leaf yellowing → Flower failure

Champaca dislikes instability.

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (TREE PHYSIOLOGY)

Champaca = Moderate feeder compared to roses/hibiscus.

Overfeeding is common mistake.

---

### 🌿 GENERAL GROWTH PHASE

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 20–30 days

Why:

✔ Balanced canopy growth  
✔ Leaf & root support

Excess nitrogen → Leaf-heavy / Flower-poor tree.

---

### 🌼 PRE-FLOWERING / FLOWERING PHASE

✔ SOP 00:00:50 (Potassium Support)

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 20–30 days

Why:

✔ Flower initiation  
✔ Fragrance intensity  
✔ Bud strength

Champaca benefits from potassium but not aggressively like hibiscus.

---

### 🦴 CALCIUM SUPPORT (OPTIONAL)

✔ Calcium Nitrate

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Bud & stem support  
✔ Overall plant strength

Do NOT mix with SOP.

---

### 🌿 MAGNESIUM BOOST (IMPORTANT)

✔ Epsom Salt

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days

Why:

✔ Leaf colour  
✔ Photosynthesis efficiency

---

### 🔬 MICRONUTRIENTS (IMPORTANT FOR FLOWERING)

✔ Micronutrient Mix

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days

Why:

✔ Prevents hidden deficiencies  
✔ Supports bud initiation  
✔ Improves overall vigor

Deficiency symptoms:

✔ Pale leaves  
✔ Poor flowering  
✔ Slow growth

---

### 🌊 SEAWEED EXTRACT (EXCELLENT)

Dose:
2–3 ml/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Stress buffering  
✔ Flower stimulation  
✔ Root vigor

Champaca responds nicely to seaweed.

---

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT

Common pests:

• Aphids  
• Mealybugs  
• Scale insects  
• Caterpillars  

Champaca generally less pest-prone than roses.

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE BASELINE

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3–5 ml/L + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 15–20 days

Increase if pests visible.

---

### ✅ SAP SUCKING PESTS

✔ Thiamethoxam (if needed)

---

### ✅ CHEWING / CATERPILLARS

✔ Chlorantraniliprole / Indoxacarb (rotate)

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Common issues:

✔ Leaf spot (rare)  
✔ Root rot (watering issue)

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE

✔ Hexaconazole

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days (humid/rainy season)

---

### ✅ CURATIVE

✔ Azoxystrobin + Mancozeb

Use when spotting visible.

---

## ✂️ PRUNING STRATEGY (MINIMALIST)

Champaca ≠ Shrub ≠ Aggressive pruning plant.

Over-pruning reduces flowering.

---

### ✅ WHEN TO PRUNE

✔ Only when necessary  
✔ After flowering season  
✔ Structural corrections only

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Dead branches  
✔ Diseased wood  
✔ Crossing branches  
✔ Weak damaged growth

---

### ✅ STRUCTURAL OBJECTIVE

✔ Naturally shaped canopy  
✔ Good airflow  
✔ Balanced branching

Champaca prefers minimal interference.

---

### ✅ CUTTING TECHNIQUE

✔ Cut just outside branch collar  
✔ Slight angled cut  
✔ Clean sharp tool  
✔ No stubs

---

## 🌼 FLOWERING FAILURE INSIGHTS

No flowers → MOST COMMON causes:

✔ Insufficient sunlight ☀️  
✔ Excess nitrogen 🌿  
✔ Plant immaturity 🌱  
✔ Root stress 💧  
✔ Potassium imbalance 🧪  
✔ Micronutrient deficiency 🔬  

Important reality:

Seed-grown champaca may take YEARS to flower.

Grafted plants flower much earlier.

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Champaca struggles mostly from:

1) Poor sunlight (MOST COMMON)  
2) Overwatering / poor drainage  
3) Excess nitrogen  
4) Impatience (expecting rapid flowering)  
5) Heavy pruning  
6) Root disturbance  
7) Nutrient deficiency

---

## 🎯 OPTIMAL CARE PHILOSOPHY

Sampige thrives on:

✔ Full sun exposure  
✔ Excellent drainage  
✔ Stable watering rhythm  
✔ Moderate balanced feeding  
✔ Potassium support (not excessive)  
✔ Periodic micronutrients  
✔ Minimal intelligent pruning  
✔ Good airflow  
✔ Patience

Champaca is a long-game plant.

It rewards stability, not intensity.

More care ≠ More flowers.

Correct care = Flowers.
