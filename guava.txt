# GUAVA CARE GUIDE
Complete nutrition + protection + pruning strategy

---

## 🌳 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Guava is one of the most forgiving yet highly responsive fruit trees.

Core traits:

✔ Fast-growing  
✔ Extremely pruning-responsive  
✔ Fruits on NEW GROWTH  
✔ Heavy feeder  
✔ Highly adaptable  
✔ Tolerates stress better than most fruit plants

Critical insight:

Guava productivity is **almost entirely controlled by pruning + nutrition**.

Neglect pruning → Yield collapses.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT

Ideal soil:

✔ Well-draining  
✔ Moderately rich  
✔ pH tolerant (5 – 8)

Use every 45–60 days:

✔ Cow Manure → 1–2 kg (tree) / 500 g (young plant)  
✔ Vermicompost → 500 g – 1 kg

Why:

✔ Sustained nutrient supply  
✔ Improves fruit flavor  
✔ Prevents micronutrient deficiency

Optional booster:

✔ Humic Acid Drench

Dose:
2–3 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Root expansion  
✔ Better nutrient uptake

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY

Young plant:

• 2–3 times per week

Established tree:

• Deep watering every 5–7 days

Golden rule:

✔ Slight drying between watering is beneficial  
✔ Avoid waterlogging

Classic mistake:

✘ Excess watering → leafy growth / poor fruiting

Mild stress = Better flowering response.

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (HIGHLY RESPONSIVE PLANT)

Guava = Heavy feeder + Frequent fruiter.

---

### 🌿 GENERAL GROWTH PHASE

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 15–20 days

Why:

✔ Balanced canopy development  
✔ Leaf & shoot growth

---

### 🍈 FLOWERING & FRUITING PHASE (MOST IMPORTANT)

✔ SOP 00:00:50

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 10–15 days

Why:

✔ Fruit size  
✔ Sweetness  
✔ Flower retention  
✔ Fruit firmness

Optimal rhythm:

Alternate:

NPK → SOP → NPK → SOP

---

Every 25–30 days

Why:

✔ Prevents fruit cracking  
✔ Improves fruit firmness  
✔ Reduces fruit drop

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT (VERY IMPORTANT FOR GUAVA)

Common pests:

• Fruit fly (major enemy)  
• Aphids  
• Mealybugs  
• Scale insects  
• Caterpillars

---

## 🚨 FRUIT FLY (BIGGEST GUAVA PROBLEM)

Symptoms:

✔ Small puncture marks  
✔ Fruits rot internally  
✔ Premature fruit drop  
✔ Maggots inside fruit

Critical behavioural rule:

✔ Fallen fruit MUST be removed immediately

Ignoring dropped fruit = Massive infestation cycle.

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE PROGRAM (BEST STRATEGY)

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3–5 ml/L + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Especially important during fruiting.

---

### ✅ SAP SUCKING PESTS

✔ Thiamethoxam

Targets:

✔ Aphids  
✔ Mealybugs  
✔ Whiteflies

Use only when infestation visible.

---

### ✅ CATERPILLARS / BORERS

✔ Chlorantraniliprole  
✔ Indoxacarb

Rotation logic:

Neem → Thiamethoxam → Indoxacarb → Back to Neem

---

### ✅ SEVERE INFESTATIONS

✔ Fipronil  
✔ Indoxacarb

Use sparingly.

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Common guava diseases:

• Leaf spot  
• Anthracnose  
• Sooty mold

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE

✔ Hexaconazole

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days (humid weather)

---

### ✅ STRONG PROTECTION

✔ Azoxystrobin + Mancozeb

Use when:

✔ Visible spotting  
✔ Leaf damage spreading  
✔ Rainy season

---

## ✂️ PRUNING STRATEGY (THIS DEFINES YOUR YIELD)

Guava fruits on **NEW SHOOTS**.

No pruning = Fewer fruits = Aging canopy.

---

### ✅ WHEN TO PRUNE (MOST IMPORTANT FACTOR)

Best timing:

✔ After harvest  
✔ Before new flush

In India typically:

Multiple pruning cycles possible per year.

---

### ✅ PRUNING INTENSITY

Guava LOVES pruning.

✔ Moderate to heavy pruning acceptable  
✔ Height control beneficial  
✔ Encourages massive fruiting wood

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Dead branches  
✔ Weak shoots  
✔ Crossing branches  
✔ Inward growth  
✔ Dense canopy areas  
✔ Excess height leaders

Goal:

✔ Open canopy  
✔ Sunlight penetration  
✔ Airflow dominance

---

### ✅ STRUCTURAL OBJECTIVE

✔ Bushy, well-branched tree  
✔ Compact fruiting framework

Tall unpruned guava = Poor harvest efficiency.

---

### ✅ CUTTING TECHNIQUE

✔ Cut above outward-facing node  
✔ Slight angled cut  
✔ Clean sharp pruner  
✔ No stubs

Wrong pruning causes:

✘ Dieback  
✘ Poor branching  
✘ Disease entry

---

## 🍈 FRUIT QUALITY INSIGHTS

Small fruits → Low potassium  
Fruit drop → Water stress / Calcium deficiency  
Leaf yellowing → Magnesium / Micronutrient deficiency  
Leafy growth / low fruit → Excess nitrogen  
Hard sour fruits → Early harvest

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Guava problems usually arise from:

1) No pruning (MOST COMMON)  
2) Fruit fly neglect  
3) Excess nitrogen  
4) Poor sunlight  
5) Waterlogging

---

## 🎯 OPTIMAL CARE PHILOSOPHY

Guava thrives on:

✔ Regular pruning cycles  
✔ Balanced NPK feeding  
✔ Potassium dominance during fruiting  
✔ Consistent watering  
✔ Aggressive fruit fly prevention  
✔ Good sunlight exposure

Guava is a productivity machine when managed correctly.

Pruning = Yield control switch.
