# CURRY LEAF CARE GUIDE 🌿  
(Murraya koenigii — Aromatic Leaf Production Tree)

---

## 🌳 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Curry leaf is a **leaf-harvest tree**, not a fruiting or ornamental flower plant.

Core traits:

✔ Leaf-production priority plant  
✔ Loves strong sunlight ☀️  
✔ Extremely nitrogen responsive 🌿  
✔ Deep-rooted once established  
✔ Hates waterlogging ❌  
✔ Very pruning responsive ✂️  
✔ Aroma quality nutrient-driven  
✔ Moderate feeder (but frequent feeding helps)

Critical mental model:

Nitrogen → Leaf growth → Aroma intensity

If leaves are small / pale → Nutrition issue  
If leaves yellow → Water/root issue

---

## ☀️ LIGHT REQUIREMENT (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Curry leaf LOVES sun.

✔ Minimum 6–8 hrs direct sunlight  
✔ Full sun ideal

Low light symptoms:

✔ Thin stems  
✔ Small leaves  
✔ Weak aroma  
✔ Leggy growth  
✔ Slow recovery

Sunlight = Leaf engine.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT (CRITICAL)

Ideal soil behaviour:

✔ Well-draining (ABSOLUTE RULE)  
✔ Loose & aerated  
✔ Moderately rich  
✔ Never compacted

BIGGEST CURRY LEAF KILLER:

❌ Wet suffocating soil → Root rot → Yellow leaves → Leaf drop

---

### ✅ ORGANIC FOUNDATION

Every 30–45 days:

✔ Vermicompost → 300–500 g  
✔ Cow Manure → Light handful

Why:

✔ Sustains leaf production  
✔ Improves aroma quality  
✔ Supports soil vitality

Optional booster:

✔ Humic Acid Drench

Dose:
2 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Root stimulation  
✔ Better nutrient absorption

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY (VERY IMPORTANT)

Golden rule:

✔ Deep watering  
✔ Allow partial drying  
✔ NEVER constant wet soil

Typical rhythm:

✔ Every 3–5 days (heat dependent)

Classic failure:

✘ Daily watering ❌

Symptoms of overwatering:

✔ Yellow leaves  
✔ Leaf drop  
✔ Weak stems  
✔ Rot smell  
✔ Slow growth

Symptoms of underwatering:

✔ Leaf droop  
✔ Slight leaf curl  
✔ Growth slowdown

Underwatering far safer.

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (LEAF PRODUCTION FOCUSED)

Curry leaf LOVES steady feeding.

---

### 🌿 PRIMARY FEEDING (MOST IMPORTANT)

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Why:

✔ Leaf size  
✔ Leaf density  
✔ Shoot growth  
✔ Aroma intensity

Nitrogen = Dominant driver here.

---

### 🌿 SOP (SUPPORTIVE ONLY)

Potassium = Secondary nutrient.

✔ Dose → 0.5 – 1 g/L  
✔ Frequency → Every 30–45 days

Why limited:

✔ Excess potassium → Smaller leaves

Leaf plants prefer Nitrogen dominance.

---

### 🦴 CALCIUM SUPPORT (OPTIONAL BUT GOOD)

✔ Calcium Nitrate

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 25–30 days

Why:

✔ Stem strength  
✔ Leaf robustness  
✔ Growth stability

---

### 🌿 MAGNESIUM BOOST (IMPORTANT)

✔ Epsom Salt

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30 days

Why:

✔ Deep green leaves  
✔ Prevents yellowing  
✔ Better photosynthesis

Magnesium deficiency VERY common.

---

### 🔬 MICRONUTRIENTS (IMPORTANT)

✔ Micronutrient Mix

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Prevents chlorosis  
✔ Sustains leaf quality  
✔ Improves aroma

Deficiency symptoms:

✔ Yellow leaves with green veins  
✔ Pale foliage  
✔ Small leaves

---

### 🌊 SEAWEED EXTRACT (EXCELLENT)

Dose:
2 ml/L

Frequency:
Every 20–30 days

Why:

✔ Stress buffering  
✔ Root vigor  
✔ Leaf thickness improvement

---

## ✂️ PRUNING STRATEGY (THIS DEFINES LEAF PRODUCTION)

Curry leaf LOVES pruning.

No pruning → Tall thin plant → Poor leaf density.

---

### ✅ WHEN TO PRUNE

✔ Anytime plant becomes leggy  
✔ After heavy harvesting  
✔ For shape & density control

---

### ✅ PRUNING OBJECTIVES

✔ Stimulate branching  
✔ Increase leaf density  
✔ Maintain compact structure  
✔ Encourage new shoots

More shoots = More leaves.

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Long thin stems  
✔ Weak growth  
✔ Crossing branches  
✔ Dead/damaged wood

---

### ✅ CUTTING TECHNIQUE (CRITICAL)

✔ Cut above leaf node  
✔ Slight angled cut  
✔ Clean sharp pruner  
✔ No stubs

Incorrect cuts → Dieback risk.

---

## 🌿 HARVESTING STRATEGY (VERY IMPORTANT)

Harvesting = Controlled pruning.

---

### ✅ CORRECT METHOD

✔ Pluck leaf stems, not individual leaves  
✔ Encourage shoot regeneration  
✔ Avoid stripping entire branch

---

### ✅ GOLDEN RULE

✔ Never remove >30–40% foliage at once

Over-harvesting = Growth shock.

---

## 🌿 COMMON CURRY LEAF PROBLEMS DECODED

Yellow leaves → MOST COMMON cause:

✔ Overwatering 💧  
✔ Poor drainage 🌱  

Small leaves → Nitrogen deficiency 🌿  
Pale leaves → Magnesium / micronutrient deficiency 🔬  
Weak aroma → Nutrition / sunlight issue ☀️  
Slow growth → Root stress / low feeding  
Leaf drop → Root stress / watering imbalance  
Leggy plant → Low sunlight / no pruning  
Very slow recovery → Cold stress / root issues  

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Curry leaf struggles mostly from:

1) Overwatering (EXTREME COMMON)  
2) Poor drainage  
3) Low nitrogen feeding  
4) Magnesium deficiency  
5) Insufficient sunlight  
6) No pruning  
7) Excess potassium  
8) Cold exposure

---

## 🎯 OPTIMAL CARE PHILOSOPHY

Curry leaf thrives on:

✔ Full sunlight dominance ☀️  
✔ Excellent drainage 🌱  
✔ Deep spaced watering 💧  
✔ Frequent nitrogen-rich feeding 🌿  
✔ Moderate magnesium support  
✔ Periodic micronutrients 🔬  
✔ Occasional potassium boost  
✔ Aggressive pruning strategy ✂️  
✔ Controlled harvesting  
✔ Warm stable environment

Curry leaf is a growth-energy plant.

Stimulate shoots → Leaf explosion  
Suppress shoots → Thin unhappy tree
