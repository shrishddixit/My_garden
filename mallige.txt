# MALLIGE (JASMINE) CARE GUIDE 🤍🌸  
(Jasminum species — Fragrance & Flower Production Plant)

---

## 🌸 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Mallige (jasmine) is a **fragrance-dominant flowering shrub/vine**.

Core traits:

✔ Flowers on NEW GROWTH  
✔ Loves sunlight (CRITICAL)  
✔ Moderate-to-heavy feeder  
✔ Highly pruning responsive  
✔ Sensitive to water stress  
✔ Extremely sensitive to overwatering  
✔ Bud production strongly nutrient-driven

Critical mental model:

Jasmine is a bud factory.

Leaf health → Shoot growth → Bud formation → Fragrance

No new shoots = No flowers.

---

## ☀️ LIGHT REQUIREMENT (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Mallige LOVES sun.

✔ Minimum 5–7 hrs direct sunlight  
✔ Morning sun excellent  
✔ Full sun ideal

Low light symptoms:

✔ Leafy plant  
✔ Few/no buds ❌  
✔ Weak fragrance  
✔ Thin stems

Sunlight = Flower trigger.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT

Ideal soil behaviour:

✔ Well-draining (CRITICAL)  
✔ Loose & aerated  
✔ Moderately rich  
✔ Never compacted

Jasmine roots HATE:

✘ Constant wet soil  
✘ Heavy sticky soil

Waterlogging = Instant flowering failure.

---

### ✅ ORGANIC FOUNDATION

Every 30–45 days:

✔ Vermicompost → 200–300 g  
✔ Cow Manure → Light handful

Why:

✔ Sustained nutrition  
✔ Improves flowering cycles  
✔ Supports microbial health

Optional booster:

✔ Humic Acid Drench

Dose:
2 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Root stimulation  
✔ Better nutrient absorption

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY (CRITICAL FOR JASMINE)

Golden rule:

✔ Allow partial drying  
✔ Never soggy soil  
✔ Avoid full drying stress

Typical rhythm:

✔ Every 2–4 days (heat dependent)

Classic jasmine killer:

✘ Daily watering ❌

Symptoms of overwatering:

✔ Yellow leaves  
✔ No buds  
✔ Leaf drop  
✔ Root stress

Underwatering symptoms:

✔ Bud drop  
✔ Leaf drooping

Consistency wins.

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (BUD PRODUCTION FOCUSED)

Mallige responds strongly to feeding precision.

---

### 🌿 GENERAL GROWTH PHASE

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Why:

✔ Leaf & shoot development  
✔ Supports new growth  
✔ Enables bud formation

---

### 🤍 FLOWERING / BUD PHASE (MOST IMPORTANT)

✔ SOP 00:00:50 (Potassium Engine)

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 10–15 days

Why:

✔ Bud formation  
✔ Flower count  
✔ Fragrance intensity  
✔ Bud retention

MOST COMMON MALLIGE FAILURE:

❌ Low potassium → Leafy plant / No buds

---

### 🦴 CALCIUM SUPPORT (IMPORTANT)

✔ Calcium Nitrate

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 25–30 days

Why:

✔ Bud strength  
✔ Stem strength  
✔ Prevents bud drop

Do NOT mix with SOP.

---

### 🌿 MAGNESIUM BOOST (VERY IMPORTANT)

✔ Epsom Salt

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30 days

Why:

✔ Deep green foliage  
✔ Improved photosynthesis  
✔ Better flowering response

Magnesium deficiency common in jasmine.

---

### 🔬 MICRONUTRIENTS (CRITICAL FOR BUDS)

✔ Micronutrient Mix

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Prevents chlorosis  
✔ Supports bud initiation  
✔ Enhances flower quality

Deficiency symptoms:

✔ Pale leaves  
✔ Small flowers  
✔ Weak buds  
✔ Poor fragrance

---

### 🌊 SEAWEED EXTRACT (EXCELLENT FOR JASMINE)

Dose:
2 ml/L

Frequency:
Every 20–30 days

Why:

✔ Bud stimulation  
✔ Stress buffering  
✔ Root vigor  
✔ Improves flowering cycles

---

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT (JASMINE = MODERATE PEST MAGNET)

Common pests:

• Aphids  
• Mealybugs  
• Thrips (VERY COMMON)  
• Spider mites  
• Bud worms  

---

## 🚨 THRIPS (MOST COMMON JASMINE ENEMY)

Symptoms:

✔ Dirty buds  
✔ Buds fail to open  
✔ Deformed flowers  
✔ Brownish petals

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE BASELINE (ESSENTIAL)

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3–5 ml/L + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Increase frequency if pests active.

---

### ✅ SAP SUCKING PESTS

✔ Thiamethoxam (only if severe)

Neem preferred when possible.

---

### ✅ CHEWING / BUD WORMS

✔ Indoxacarb / Chlorantraniliprole (rotate)

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Common diseases:

✔ Leaf spot  
✔ Powdery mildew  
✔ Root rot (watering issue)

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE

✔ Hexaconazole

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days (humid/rainy season)

---

### ✅ CURATIVE

✔ Azoxystrobin + Mancozeb

Use when spotting visible.

---

## ✂️ PRUNING STRATEGY (THIS DEFINES FLOWERING)

Mallige flowers on NEW GROWTH.

Old wood = Flowering decline.

---

### ✅ WHEN TO PRUNE

✔ After flowering flush  
✔ When plant becomes leggy  
✔ Seasonal shaping

Jasmine LOVES pruning.

---

### ✅ PRUNING OBJECTIVES

✔ Stimulate new shoots  
✔ Increase bud sites  
✔ Control structure  
✔ Improve airflow

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Weak thin stems  
✔ Long leggy branches  
✔ Crossing branches  
✔ Inward growth  
✔ Dead/diseased wood

Goal:

✔ Bushy structure  
✔ Many growing tips

More tips = More buds.

---

### ✅ PRUNING INTENSITY

✔ Moderate pruning very beneficial  
✔ Hard pruning tolerated by mature plants

---

### ✅ CUTTING TECHNIQUE

✔ Cut above leaf node  
✔ Slight angled cut  
✔ Clean pruner  
✔ No stubs

---

## 🤍 COMMON MALLIGE PROBLEMS DECODED

Leafy plant, no buds → Excess nitrogen / Low potassium / Low sunlight  
Bud drop → Water stress / Potassium deficiency  
Small flowers → Potassium deficiency  
Weak fragrance → Low sunlight / Poor nutrition  
Yellow leaves → Overwatering / Magnesium deficiency  
Dirty buds → Thrips  
Buds forming but not opening → Thrips / Stress  
Slow growth → Root stress / Nutrient depletion

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Mallige struggles mostly from:

1) Overwatering (MOST COMMON)  
2) Low sunlight  
3) Potassium deficiency  
4) No pruning  
5) Magnesium deficiency  
6) Thrips infestation  
7) Poor drainage

---

## 🎯 OPTIMAL CARE PHILOSOPHY

Mallige thrives on:

✔ Strong sunlight exposure  
✔ Excellent drainage  
✔ Balanced feeding rhythm  
✔ Potassium-rich bud support  
✔ Regular magnesium supplementation  
✔ Periodic micronutrients  
✔ Stable watering rhythm  
✔ Neem-based prevention  
✔ Frequent pruning for new shoots

Mallige is a growth-cycle plant.

Encourage new shoots → You unlock flowers.

Suppress new shoots → Flowering stalls.
