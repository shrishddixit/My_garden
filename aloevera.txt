# ALOE VERA CARE GUIDE 🌿  
(Succulent physiology — Minimalist feeding strategy)

---

## 🌵 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Aloe vera is a succulent, not a typical leafy plant.

Core traits:

✔ Stores water in leaves  
✔ Extremely drought tolerant  
✔ Sensitive to overwatering (BIGGEST KILLER)  
✔ Low nutrient demand  
✔ Thrives on neglect more than care  
✔ Shallow root system  
✔ Slow metabolic rate

Critical mental model:

Aloe vera is biologically designed for scarcity.

Over-care = Primary cause of death.

---

## ☀️ LIGHT REQUIREMENT (VERY IMPORTANT)

Ideal exposure:

✔ Bright light  
✔ Partial to full sun  
✔ Indoor bright window OK  
✔ Shade ❌ problematic

Symptoms of low light:

✔ Long stretched leaves  
✔ Pale green colour  
✔ Weak floppy growth

Too much harsh sun (rare issue):

✔ Leaf burn patches

Aloe tolerates sun better than most garden plants.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT (CRITICAL)

Soil MUST drain aggressively.

Ideal soil behaviour:

✔ Fast draining  
✔ Sandy / gritty  
✔ Aerated  
✔ Never water-retentive

Aloe hates:

✘ Heavy soil  
✘ Compacted soil  
✘ Constant moisture

If soil stays wet → Root rot → Plant collapse.

---

### ✅ ORGANIC INPUT (VERY LIGHT)

Every 60–90 days ONLY:

✔ Vermicompost → Small pinch  
✔ Cow Manure → Very tiny amount (optional)

Why minimal feeding:

✔ Aloe is low nutrient consumer  
✔ Excess nutrients → Soft weak growth → Rot risk

Most aloe problems come from excess fertilizer.

---

### ✅ HUMIC ACID (OPTIONAL BUT SAFE)

Dose:
2 g/L

Frequency:
Every 60 days

Why:

✔ Root health  
✔ Soil balance  
✔ Helps container soil

Not essential but beneficial.

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY (MOST IMPORTANT RULE)

Golden rule:

✔ Water deeply  
✔ Then IGNORE until fully dry

Typical rhythm:

✔ Every 10–20 days (depends on heat)

Test method (best practice):

✔ Insert finger deep  
✔ Water ONLY if completely dry

Classic aloe killer:

✘ Frequent watering

Symptoms of overwatering:

✔ Mushy base  
✔ Yellow leaves  
✔ Sudden collapse  
✔ Rot smell

Symptoms of underwatering (rarely serious):

✔ Slight leaf thinning  
✔ Minor wrinkling

Underwatering is far safer than overwatering.

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (MINIMALIST)

Aloe ≠ Feeding-hungry plant.

---

### ✅ PRIMARY FEEDING (LIGHT & RARE)

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
0.5 g/L (VERY LIGHT)

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days

Why:

✔ Gentle growth support  
✔ Prevents depletion in pots

---

### 🌿 MAGNESIUM BOOST (OCCASIONAL)

✔ Epsom Salt

Dose:
0.5 g/L

Frequency:
Every 60–90 days

Why:

✔ Leaf colour support

---

### 🔬 MICRONUTRIENTS (LOW PRIORITY)

✔ Micronutrient Mix

Frequency:
Every 60–90 days (optional)

Only needed if:

✔ Pale growth  
✔ Slow recovery  
✔ Poor leaf colour

---

### 🚨 SOP (RARELY NEEDED)

Potassium rarely limiting for aloe.

Avoid routine SOP use.

---

### 🌊 SEAWEED EXTRACT (OPTIONAL)

Dose:
2 ml/L

Frequency:
Every 45–60 days

Why:

✔ Stress recovery  
✔ Root stimulation

Not required but safe.

---

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT

Aloe is naturally pest-resistant.

Occasional pests:

✔ Mealybugs (MOST COMMON)  
✔ Aphids (rare)  
✔ Scale insects (rare)

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE BASELINE

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3 ml/L

Frequency:
Only when pests seen OR once monthly preventive

Avoid excessive spraying.

---

### ✅ MEALYBUG CONTROL (IMPORTANT)

Symptoms:

✔ White cottony clusters  
✔ Base / leaf joints

Fix:

✔ Neem Oil  
✔ Manual wiping (VERY effective)

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Aloe disease = Almost always watering related.

Most common:

✔ Root rot  
✔ Stem rot

Cause:

✘ Overwatering  
✘ Poor drainage

Fungicide rarely needed if watering corrected.

---

## ✂️ PRUNING / MAINTENANCE STRATEGY

Aloe pruning ≠ Structural pruning.

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Dead leaves  
✔ Damaged leaves  
✔ Rotting sections  
✔ Old dried growth

---

### ✅ HOW TO CUT

✔ Clean sharp blade  
✔ Cut at base  
✔ Avoid tearing

---

### ✅ PUP MANAGEMENT (VERY IMPORTANT)

Aloe produces offsets ("pups").

✔ Can be separated  
✔ Prevent overcrowding  
✔ Controls nutrient competition

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Aloe vera struggles mostly from:

1) Overwatering (EXTREME COMMON KILLER)  
2) Poor drainage  
3) Excess fertilizer  
4) Low light (indoor setups)  
5) Dense compacted soil

Not from neglect.

Neglect usually improves aloe health.

---

## 🎯 OPTIMAL CARE PHILOSOPHY

Aloe thrives on:

✔ Bright light  
✔ Aggressive drainage  
✔ Deep but rare watering  
✔ Minimal feeding  
✔ Excellent airflow  
✔ Occasional maintenance only

Think:

Desert survivor, not tropical leaf plant.

Less care = More success.
