# SAPOTA (CHIKOO) CARE GUIDE
Comprehensive usage of your available products + pruning strategy

---

## 🌳 Plant Nature & Growth Behavior

Sapota is a slow-growing evergreen tropical fruit tree.

Key biological traits:

• Deep-rooted, drought tolerant once established  
• Sensitive to waterlogging (roots suffocate easily)  
• Heavy feeder during fruiting cycles  
• Minimal pruning required compared to ornamentals  
• Responds well to balanced nutrition + potassium

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT

Ideal soil:

• Well-draining (CRITICAL)  
• Slightly acidic to neutral (pH 6.0 – 7.5)  
• Rich in organic matter

If soil is heavy / clayey:

→ Mix vermicompost + manure to improve aeration

Use:

• TrustBasket Cow Manure  
• Coir Vermicompost  
• Humic Acid (Huminoz-98)

Application (every 45–60 days):

✔ Cow manure → 1–2 kg around drip circle  
✔ Vermicompost → 500 g – 1 kg  
✔ Humic Acid → Soil drench (see below)

Humic Acid Drench:

Dose:
2–3 g per liter water

Purpose:
✔ Improves nutrient absorption  
✔ Stimulates root system  
✔ Reduces transplant stress

Frequency:
Once every 30–45 days

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY

Young Plant:

• 2–3 times per week

Established Tree:

• Deep watering once weekly

Golden Rule:

✔ Soil should dry slightly between watering  
✔ NEVER allow standing water  
✔ Overwatering → root rot → leaf yellowing

Common mistake:
Frequent shallow watering → weak roots

Correct method:
Slow deep soak

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT

Common sapota pests:

• Mealybugs  
• Aphids  
• Leaf miners  
• Scale insects  
• Caterpillars

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE PROGRAM (Ideal)

Neem Oil

Dose:
3–5 ml per liter + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Why:
✔ Soft pest suppression  
✔ Safe baseline protection  
✔ Prevents infestations

---

### ✅ SAP SUCKING PESTS

Use:

✔ Thiamethoxam (Thiolt-30)

Dose:
As label (usually 0.3–0.5 ml/L)

Frequency:
Only when pests observed

Why:
✔ Aphids  
✔ Jassids  
✔ Mealybugs (early stage)

---

### ✅ CHEWING / BORING / CATERPILLARS

✔ Chlorantraniliprole (Clorentis)

Why:
✔ Caterpillars  
✔ Leaf eaters  
✔ Borers

---

### ✅ HEAVY INFESTATION / RESISTANT PESTS

✔ Indoxacarb  
✔ Fipronil  
✔ Novaluron (growth regulator)

Rotation logic (CRITICAL):

DO NOT repeat same chemical repeatedly.

Rotate:

Neem → Thiamethoxam → Indoxacarb → Back to Neem

Prevents resistance.

Sapota diseases:

Hexaconazole 5% SC

Every 30–45 days in humid/wet season

### ✅ BROAD SPECTRUM PROTECTION

Azoxystrobin + Mancozeb

## ✂️ PRUNING STRATEGY (Sapota Specific)

Sapota ≠ Rose ≠ Aggressive pruning plant

Golden principle:

✔ Minimal structural pruning  
✔ Remove problems, not growth

---

### ✅ WHEN TO PRUNE

Best timing:

✔ After harvest cycle  
✔ Before active growth flush

Avoid:

✘ Peak flowering  
✘ Heavy fruiting stage

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Dead branches  
✔ Diseased wood  
✔ Crossing branches  
✔ Inward-growing branches  
✔ Suckers at base

---

### ✅ STRUCTURAL SHAPING (Young Tree)

Goal:

Open canopy → light penetration → better fruiting

Remove:

✔ Crowded interior shoots  
✔ Weak competing leaders

Maintain:

✔ Strong scaffold branches  
✔ Airflow corridors

---

### ✅ CUTTING METHOD (CRITICAL DETAIL)

Wrong Cut → Disease entry → Branch dieback

Correct Cut:

✔ Use sharp clean pruner  
✔ Cut just outside branch collar  
✔ Slight angled cut  
✔ NO stub left behind

After thick cuts:

✔ Optional fungicide dab

---

### ✅ COMMON PRUNING MISTAKES

✘ Over-pruning → fruit reduction  
✘ Flat cuts → water accumulation  
✘ Dirty tools → fungal infection

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Sapota killers (most common):

1) Overwatering  
2) Poor drainage  
3) Excess nitrogen → leafy tree / low fruit  
4) No potassium → small / tasteless fruits  
5) No pest control → sooty mold

---

## 🎯 OPTIMAL CARE PHILOSOPHY

Sapota thrives on:

✔ Stable watering  
✔ Strong organic base  
✔ Balanced NPK  
✔ Potassium dominance during fruiting  
✔ Light intelligent pruning

Sapota rewards patience more than force.

Slow tree → Long life → Heavy yield.
