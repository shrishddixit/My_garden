# SABBASGI (DILL) CARE GUIDE 🌿❄️  
(Dill / Anethum graveolens — Aromatic Leaf Herb)

---

## 🌿 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Sabbasgi is a **cool-season aromatic herb**, somewhat like coriander but with its own personality.

Core traits:

✔ Fast lifecycle ⚡  
✔ Cool-season preference ❄️  
✔ EXTREMELY bolt-prone 🚨  
✔ Delicate root system  
✔ Light feeder  
✔ Aroma quality nutrition-driven  
✔ Feathery foliage growth  
✔ Sensitive to heat ☀️🚨  
✔ Sensitive to water stress 💧  

Critical mental model:

Grow fast → Harvest early → Replant often 🌿

Delay → Tall flower stalk → Leaf phase ends 😄

---

## ☀️ LIGHT REQUIREMENT (CRITICAL BALANCE)

Dill LOVES brightness but hates harsh heat.

✔ Bright light ✅  
✔ Mild morning sun ☀️✅  
✔ Harsh afternoon sun ❌  

Ideal condition:

✔ Cool bright environment ❄️  

Heat stress symptoms:

✔ Rapid bolting 🚨  
✔ Weak aroma  
✔ Thin leaves  
✔ Growth collapse

Low light symptoms:

✔ Leggy stems  
✔ Pale foliage  
✔ Poor flavour

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT

Ideal soil behaviour:

✔ Loose & airy  
✔ Excellent drainage 🚨  
✔ Moderately rich  
✔ Moisture-retentive but NOT soggy

BIGGEST FAILURE MODE:

❌ Poor drainage → Root stress  
❌ Nutrient-heavy soil → Early bolting 🚨

---

### ✅ ORGANIC FOUNDATION (LIGHT)

Before sowing / Every 20–25 days:

✔ Vermicompost → Light mixing  
✔ Cow Manure → Very light mixing

Why light feeding:

✔ Dill = Light feeder  
✔ Excess nitrogen accelerates bolting 🚨  

Optional booster:

✔ Humic Acid Drench

Dose:
2 g/L

Frequency:
Once early stage

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY (VERY IMPORTANT 🚨)

Golden rule:

✔ Soil consistently moist ✅  
✔ NEVER bone dry ❌  
✔ NEVER soggy ❌

Typical rhythm:

✔ Light watering daily OR alternate day

Critical insight:

Water stress = Instant bolting trigger 🚨🚨

---

### 🚨 SYMPTOMS DECODER

Underwatering:

✔ Leaf droop  
✔ Rapid bolting 🚨  
✔ Aroma decline

Overwatering:

✔ Yellow foliage  
✔ Weak stems  
✔ Root stress

Consistency = Tender aromatic leaves 🌿

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (MINIMALIST 🚨)

Dill ≠ Heavy feeder.

Overfeeding = Classic mistake.

---

### 🌿 PRIMARY FEEDING (LIGHT ONLY)

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
0.5 g/L

Frequency:
Every 12–15 days

Why:

✔ Leaf colour  
✔ Growth stability  
✔ Aroma quality 🌿

---

## 🚨 CRITICAL WARNING

❌ Excess nitrogen → Fast bolting 🚨🚨

---

### 🌿 SOP (OPTIONAL / LOW PRIORITY)

✔ Dose → 0.5 g/L  
✔ Frequency → Once mid-lifecycle

Why:

✔ Stress tolerance  
✔ Leaf structure balance

Low priority.

---

### 🌿 MAGNESIUM BOOST (BENEFICIAL)

✔ Epsom Salt

Dose:
0.5 g/L

Frequency:
Once/twice lifecycle

Why:

✔ Deep green foliage  
✔ Aroma enhancement

---

### 🔬 MICRONUTRIENTS (OPTIONAL)

✔ Micronutrient Mix

Frequency:
If leaves pale

---

### 🌊 SEAWEED EXTRACT (EXCELLENT)

Dose:
2 ml/L

Frequency:
Once/twice lifecycle

Why:

✔ Root vigor  
✔ Stress buffering  
✔ Aroma enhancement 🌿

---

## ✂️ HARVESTING STRATEGY (CRITICAL 🚨)

Dill harvesting = Timing-sensitive.

---

### ✅ CORRECT METHOD

✔ Harvest young leaves early ✂️  
✔ Cut outer foliage  
✔ Avoid delaying harvest ❌

---

### 🚨 COMMON FAILURE

❌ Waiting too long → Bolting → Leaf production ends 😄

---

## 🌼 BOLTING (BIGGEST DILL DRAMA 🚨🚨)

Dill bolts due to:

✔ Heat ☀️  
✔ Water stress 💧  
✔ Nitrogen excess 🚨  
✔ Aging lifecycle  
✔ Stress conditions

Bolting effects:

❌ Leaves sparse  
❌ Aroma changes  
❌ Leaf phase ends

Solution:

✔ Harvest early  
✔ Replant frequently 🌿

---

## 🌿 SOWING STRATEGY (SMART GARDENER TRICK 😄)

✔ Sow small batches every 2–3 weeks

Result:

✔ Continuous sabbasgi supply ✨

---

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT

Common pests:

✔ Aphids  
✔ Caterpillars 🐛  
✔ Leaf miners  

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE BASELINE

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3 ml/L + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days OR when pests seen

Neem usually sufficient.

---

### ✅ CHEWING PESTS

✔ Indoxacarb (rarely needed)

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Occasional issues:

✔ Leaf spot  
✔ Root rot (watering issue)

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE

✔ Hexaconazole

Only if fungal symptoms visible.

Rarely required.

---

## 🌿 COMMON DILL PROBLEMS DECODED

Sudden tall stem → Bolting 🚨😄  
Weak aroma → Low nutrition / Heat stress  
Thin leaves → Heat stress ☀️  
Yellow foliage → Overwatering 💧  
Rapid bolting → Heat / Nitrogen excess 🚨  
Leggy growth → Low light  
Leaf bitterness → Heat stress  
Slow growth → Soil depletion  
Sparse leaves → Aging lifecycle  

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Dill struggles mostly from:

1) Heat stress (MOST COMMON FAILURE) ☀️🚨  
2) Water stress 💧🚨  
3) Nitrogen excess 🚨  
4) Delayed harvesting  
5) Poor drainage  
6) Overcrowding  
7) Natural short lifecycle 😄

---

Sabbasgi thrives on:

✔ Cool bright environment ❄️  
✔ Mild sunlight ☀️  
✔ Loose airy soil 🌱

Grow → Cut → Enjoy → Replant 🌿✨
