# GREEN PEAS CARE GUIDE 🌿🟢  
(Peas / Pisum sativum — Cool-Season Climbing Vegetable)

---

## 🌿 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Green peas are a **cool-weather nitrogen-fixing legume**, completely different from heat crops like chilli/bhindi.

Core traits:

✔ Cool-season plant ❄️  
✔ Sensitive to heat stress ☀️🚨  
✔ Moderate feeder (LIGHT feeding required)  
✔ Nitrogen-fixing 🌿  
✔ Requires support/trellis ✂️  
✔ Very flowering-sensitive  
✔ Shallow root system  
✔ Short lifecycle crop ⚡  
✔ Pest-prone 🐛  

Critical mental model:

Cool environment + Light nutrition → Flower → Pods 🟢

Heat + Excess nitrogen → Leafy disappointment ❌

---

## ☀️ LIGHT REQUIREMENT

Ideal exposure:

✔ Bright sunlight ✅  
✔ Mild direct sun ☀️  
✔ Avoid harsh afternoon heat 🚨  

Peas LOVE:

✔ Cool bright conditions

Heat stress symptoms:

✔ Flower drop 🌼❌  
✔ Poor pod formation  
✔ Rapid decline  
✔ Small weak plants

Temperature matters more than sunlight intensity.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT (CRITICAL)

Ideal soil behaviour:

✔ Loose & aerated  
✔ Excellent drainage  
✔ Moderately rich soil  
✔ NOT nutrient-heavy

Peas roots DISLIKE:

❌ Excess nitrogen  
❌ Heavy fertilization

Why:

✔ Nitrogen excess suppresses flowering

---

### ✅ ORGANIC FOUNDATION (LIGHT)

Before sowing / Every 25–30 days:

✔ Vermicompost → Light mixing  
✔ Cow Manure → Very light mixing

Why light feeding:

✔ Peas fix their own nitrogen 🌿  
✔ Excess nutrients harmful

Optional booster:

✔ Humic Acid Drench

Dose:
2 g/L

Frequency:
Every 25–30 days

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY (VERY IMPORTANT)

Golden rule:

✔ Soil consistently moist ✅  
✔ NEVER bone dry ❌  
✔ NEVER soggy ❌

Typical rhythm:

✔ Light watering daily OR alternate day

Critical insight:

Peas roots shallow → Dry quickly → Flower drop risk.

Underwatering symptoms:

✔ Leaf droop  
✔ Flower drop 🌼❌  
✔ Small pods

Overwatering symptoms:

✔ Yellow leaves  
✔ Root stress

Consistency = Pod quality.

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (CRITICAL DIFFERENCE)

Peas ≠ Heavy feeder vegetable.

LIGHT feeding required.

---

### 🌿 PRIMARY FEEDING (LIGHT)

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
0.5 g/L (LIGHT)

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Why:

✔ Balanced support  
✔ Avoids nutrient stress

High doses unnecessary 🚨

---

### 🌼 FLOWERING & POD PHASE (MOST IMPORTANT)

✔ SOP 00:00:50 (CRITICAL)

Dose:
0.5 – 1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Why:

✔ Flower retention 🌼  
✔ Pod formation 🟢  
✔ Pod filling  
✔ Stress tolerance

Potassium = Pod stabilizer.

---

## ✂️ STRUCTURAL MANAGEMENT (IMPORTANT)

Peas = Natural climbers.

---

### ✅ SUPPORT SYSTEM (MANDATORY)

✔ Trellis / Net / Strings

Without support:

✔ Tangled vines  
✔ Poor airflow  
✔ Disease risk  
✔ Lower yield

---

### ✅ LIGHT VINE MANAGEMENT

✔ Remove damaged leaves  
✔ Prevent excessive overcrowding

Dense canopy → Disease explosion ❌

---

## 🌼 FLOWERING FAILURE INSIGHTS

Flowers dropping → MOST COMMON causes:

✔ Heat stress ☀️🚨 (MOST COMMON)  
✔ Excess nitrogen 🌿  
✔ Water stress 💧  
✔ Potassium deficiency 🧪  
✔ Nutrient imbalance  
✔ Pest stress 🐛  

Temperature instability = Major killer.

---

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT

Common pests:

• Aphids 🚨🚨  
• Thrips  
• Pod borers 🐛  
• Leaf miners  
• Mites  

---

## 🚨 APHIDS (VERY COMMON PEAS ENEMY)

Symptoms:

✔ Leaf curling  
✔ Sticky leaves  
✔ Weak growth  
✔ Flower drop 🌼❌  

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE BASELINE (ESSENTIAL)

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3–5 ml/L + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 7–10 days

---

### ✅ SAP SUCKERS (IF SEVERE)

✔ Thiamethoxam

Use when infestation visible.

---

### ✅ CHEWING / BORER CONTROL

✔ Indoxacarb / Chlorantraniliprole

Rotate chemistry.

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Common diseases:

✔ Powdery mildew 🚨  
✔ Downy mildew  
✔ Wilt diseases  

Peas sensitive to fungal stress.

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE FUNGICIDE

✔ Hexaconazole

Frequency:
Every 15–20 days (humid/cool season)

---

### ✅ STRONG PROTECTION

✔ Azoxystrobin + Mancozeb

Use when spotting visible.

---

## 🌿 COMMON PEAS PROBLEMS DECODED

Lots of leaves, few pods → Excess nitrogen 🚨  
Flowers dropping → Heat stress ☀️🚨  
Small pods → Potassium deficiency  
Yellow leaves → Overwatering / Stress  
Leaf curl → Aphids 🐛  
Weak vines → Low light / Poor soil  
Pods not filling → Potassium deficiency  
Powder on leaves → Powdery mildew 🍄  
Rapid plant death → Heat stress  
Sparse pods → Nutrient imbalance  

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Peas struggle mostly from:

1) Heat stress (MOST COMMON FAILURE) ☀️🚨  
2) Excess nitrogen  
3) Water stress  
4) Potassium deficiency  
5) No support system  
6) Aphid infestation 🐛🚨  
7) Poor airflow  
8) Fungal disease 🍄  
9) Nutrient starvation (less severe vs tomato)

---
