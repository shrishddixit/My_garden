# SUGANDHARAJA CARE GUIDE 🌼  
(Tuberose / Polianthes tuberosa — Fragrance & Flower Production Plant)

---

## 🌿 Plant Nature & Behaviour Model

Sugandharaja (tuberose) is a bulbous flowering plant.

Core traits:

✔ Grows from bulbs  
✔ Loves sunlight (VERY IMPORTANT)  
✔ Heavy feeder during flowering  
✔ Sensitive to waterlogging  
✔ Responds strongly to potassium  
✔ Requires seasonal maintenance  
✔ Produces flower spikes (not bush flowers)

Critical mental model:

Bulb = Energy storage organ.

Leaf growth feeds bulb → Bulb feeds flowers.

Bad nutrition → Weak spikes → Poor fragrance.

---

## ☀️ LIGHT REQUIREMENT (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Sugandharaja LOVES sun.

✔ Minimum 6–8 hrs direct sunlight  
✔ Full sun ideal

Low light symptoms:

✔ Thin weak leaves  
✔ No flower spikes  
✔ Small blooms  
✔ Poor fragrance

This plant is sunlight addicted.

---

## 🌱 SOIL MANAGEMENT (CRITICAL FOR BULBS)

Ideal soil:

✔ Very well-draining  
✔ Loose & aerated  
✔ Rich but not heavy  
✔ Never compacted

Bulb killer:

✘ Waterlogged soil → Bulb rot → Sudden collapse

---

### ✅ ORGANIC FOUNDATION

Every 30–45 days:

✔ Vermicompost → 200–300 g  
✔ Cow Manure → Light handful

Why:

✔ Sustained feeding  
✔ Supports bulb energy storage  
✔ Improves spike strength

Optional booster:

✔ Humic Acid Drench

Dose:
2 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Root stimulation  
✔ Better nutrient uptake

---

## 💧 WATERING STRATEGY

Golden rule:

✔ Moist — NOT wet  
✔ Allow slight drying between watering

Typical rhythm:

✔ Every 2–4 days (depends on heat)

Classic failure:

✘ Constant wet soil → Bulb rot

Symptoms of overwatering:

✔ Yellowing leaves  
✔ Soft mushy base  
✔ Sudden plant collapse

Underwatering symptoms:

✔ Leaf tip drying  
✔ Smaller spikes

---

## 🧪 FERTILIZER PROGRAM (FLOWERING PERFORMANCE DRIVEN)

Sugandharaja = Heavy feeder during active growth.

---

### 🌿 GENERAL GROWTH PHASE

✔ NPK 19:19:19

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Why:

✔ Leaf growth  
✔ Bulb strengthening  
✔ Root support

Healthy leaves = Strong flowers later.

---

### 🌼 FLOWERING PHASE (MOST IMPORTANT)

Shift emphasis → Potassium dominant

✔ SOP 00:00:50

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 10–15 days

Why:

✔ Spike formation  
✔ Flower count  
✔ Fragrance intensity  
✔ Bloom longevity

Potassium = Flower engine.

---

### 🦴 CALCIUM SUPPORT (IMPORTANT)

✔ Calcium Nitrate

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 25–30 days

Why:

✔ Spike strength  
✔ Flower durability  
✔ Prevents bending spikes

Do NOT mix with SOP.

---

### 🌿 MAGNESIUM BOOST

✔ Epsom Salt

Dose:
1 g/L

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Why:

✔ Leaf colour  
✔ Photosynthesis support  
✔ Bulb energy efficiency

---

### 🔬 MICRONUTRIENTS

✔ Micronutrient Mix

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days

Deficiency symptoms:

✔ Weak spikes  
✔ Pale leaves  
✔ Small flowers  
✔ Poor fragrance

---

### 🌊 SEAWEED EXTRACT (EXCELLENT FOR SPIKES)

Dose:
2–3 ml/L

Frequency:
Every 20–30 days

Why:

✔ Flower stimulation  
✔ Stress buffering  
✔ Root vigor

---

## 🐛 PEST MANAGEMENT

Common pests:

• Aphids  
• Thrips (VERY COMMON in tuberose)  
• Mealybugs  
• Mites

---

## 🚨 THRIPS (MAJOR ENEMY)

Symptoms:

✔ Distorted flowers  
✔ Poor opening  
✔ Silvery streaks  
✔ Weak spikes

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE BASELINE (ESSENTIAL)

✔ Neem Oil

Dose:
3–5 ml/L + Wetting agent

Frequency:
Every 10–14 days

Critical during flowering cycles.

---

### ✅ SAP SUCKING / THRIPS CONTROL

✔ Thiamethoxam (if severe)

Use only when needed.

---

### ✅ CHEWING / HIDDEN PESTS

✔ Chlorantraniliprole  
✔ Indoxacarb

Rotate chemistry.

---

## 🍄 DISEASE MANAGEMENT

Common issues:

✔ Bulb rot (MOST COMMON)  
✔ Leaf spot  
✔ Fungal wilt

---

### ✅ PREVENTIVE

✔ Hexaconazole

Frequency:
Every 30–45 days (humid conditions)

---

### ✅ CURATIVE / STRONG PROTECTION

✔ Azoxystrobin + Mancozeb

Use when spotting visible.

---

## ✂️ PRUNING / MAINTENANCE STRATEGY

Sugandharaja pruning = Spike & bulb management.

---

### ✅ AFTER FLOWERING

✔ Remove spent spikes  
✔ Allow leaves to remain (CRITICAL)

Why:

Leaves recharge bulbs.

Cutting leaves early = Weak next cycle.

---

### ✅ WHAT TO REMOVE

✔ Dead leaves  
✔ Yellow leaves  
✔ Finished flower spikes

---

### ✅ BULB MANAGEMENT (VERY IMPORTANT)

Over time:

✔ Bulbs multiply  
✔ Soil overcrowding occurs  
✔ Flowering declines

Every 1–2 years:

✔ Lift bulbs  
✔ Divide  
✔ Replant

---

## 🌼 FLOWERING FAILURE INSIGHTS

No spikes → Low sunlight / Excess nitrogen  
Small spikes → Potassium deficiency  
Weak bending spikes → Calcium deficiency  
Poor fragrance → Potassium imbalance / Low light  
Yellow leaves → Overwatering / Magnesium deficiency  
Sudden collapse → Bulb rot (watering issue)

---

## ⚠️ HIGH-IMPACT FAILURE POINTS

Sugandharaja struggles mostly from:

1) Poor drainage (BULB ROT KING)  
2) Insufficient sunlight  
3) Potassium deficiency  
4) Excess nitrogen → Leaves only  
5) Cutting leaves too early  
6) Thrips infestation

---

## 🎯 OPTIMAL CARE PHILOSOPHY

Sugandharaja thrives on:

✔ Full sunlight dominance  
✔ Excellent drainage  
✔ Balanced NPK feeding  
✔ Potassium-rich flowering support  
✔ Stable moisture rhythm  
✔ Regular spike removal  
✔ Bulb division cycles  
✔ Preventive neem protection

Healthy leaves today = Fragrant spikes tomorrow.

Bulb economy never lies.
